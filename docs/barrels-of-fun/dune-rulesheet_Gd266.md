# Rules Overview
Power Over Spice… Is Power Over Everything

Based on the Academy Award-winning film franchise from Legendary Entertainment, step into the world of Arrakis and develop your skills to fight alongside the Fremen and House Atreides against the dreaded Harkonnen army! Through day and night, do you have what it takes to survive this unforgiving desert planet and harness its power to the fullest?

# Skill Shots
The plunger on this game is player-controlled. Plunging at full strength will usually result in the ball completing the orbit.

## Regular Skill Shots
Skill shots are selectable with the right flipper prior to the plunge. The currently selected skill shot will appear with an orange arrow. To collect the award, no other switch may be triggered. **The skill shot value and award are both doubled if you do not flip before making the shot.**

1. **Scoop (Front):** Collect 10 water/spice - 2,500,000 pts (5,000,000 if doubled, awards 20 resources)
2. **Sneak (Rear Scoop):** Collect 5 water/spice - 5,000,000 pts (10,000,000 if doubled, awards 10 resources)
3. **Left Spinner:** Light all water/spice - 7,500,000 pts (15,000,000 if doubled)

The resource type awarded depends on the current time period: water during day, spice during night.

## Secret Skill Shots
These are not selectable - just hit them directly from the plunge:

1. **Tunnel:** 10,000,000 pts - Spinners generate more resources for the rest of the ball
2. **Left Outlane:** 5,000,000 pts - Immediately load Sand Snork and autoplunge another ball
3. **Center Spinner (Dribble):** 2,500,000 pts - "Satisfaction!"
4. **Upper Right Ramp (Side Ramp):** 5,000,000 pts - "Satisfaction!"

# Day & Night Cycle
The game cycles between day and night. The HAL lighting will be orange (day) or blue (night). You can track the advancement of the current cycle on the half moon at the top of the LCD screen. Each switch hit (on switches tagged for time progression) will make progress towards the next cycle. By default, a day/night lasts **200 switch hits** (configurable in settings).

You can play one mode per cycle:
- **Day:** Harvester Battle
- **Night:** Prophecy Mode

## Time Progression Details
- **Acceleration:** During day, if a Harvester Battle has been started this cycle, time progresses at **2x speed**. During night, if a Prophecy mode has been started, time progresses at **2x speed**.
- **Hold Point:** At 80% of the cycle, if time is prevented (e.g., during a running mode), progress pauses until the mode ends. This prevents the cycle from changing mid-mode.
- **Pending Modifiers:** Some Bene Gesserit awards (super spinners, double resources) are "pending" and activate at the start of the next appropriate cycle.
- **Starting Time Period:** Configurable - always day, always night, alternate per game, or random 50/50.

# Resources
There are 2 resources in the game: Water & Spice. The current quantities collected are tracked at the upper left & right respectively of the LCD screen. Both resources cap at **100** by default.

Small dots in front of each shot (blue for Water, orange for Spice) represent collectable resources. Making a shot will collect all resources in front of it. There are **5 resource dot clusters** mapped to: left ramp, left orbit, center, pit, and right orbit.

To spread resources on the playfield, you need to activate spinners by hitting the **left inlane** when lit. Once active, each spinner will be lit (blue for Water, orange for Spice) and each spin will add a single resource dot to the playfield. The left inlane also lights **2 random unlit targets** (from the 5 standup targets 3–7) with the **opposite** resource type.

Each resource collected during the game will be tallied during the end of ball bonus.

**Resource Carryover:** By default (configurable), uncollected lit water/spice on the playfield carries over ball to ball.

**Tilt Penalty:** All water and spice are set to **0** on tilt.

## Water
Generated at night. Water will be consumed during a Harvester Battle, acting as a mode timer. Once water has run out, the battle will end. Water remaining also increases the sacrifice jackpot value. Each water collected scores **50,000 pts**. Bonus value: **13,000 per water** collected during the ball.

## Spice
Generated during the day. All spice will be consumed when reaching the cash-out decision in a Prophecy mode, increasing its jackpot value by **1,000,000 for each spice** consumed (minimum jackpot: 5,000,000). Each spice collected scores **50,000 pts**. Bonus value: **22,000 per spice** collected during the ball.

# Items
Items can be collected by hitting the 3 drop targets next to the upper flipper.

The active item can be changed by using the **right flipper**, cycling from top to bottom.

First drop target hit will lock the item to be collected. You need to drop them all to successfully collect it. They will reset after a timeout (20 seconds). If all 3 are knocked down within 1 second, it counts as a "sweep."

After collecting the first item of a type, you will need to collect the other 2 item types before being able to collect another of the same type.

## Sand Snork
Left outlane ball save. Load/enable at the yellow-lit left ramp once collected from the drops. An unused loaded Snork will be ejected at the end of your ball, but you'll begin next ball with lit left ramp for easy reload. On default settings, you can stack up to **3 Snorks** in your inventory (configurable).

## Lasgun
Blue shot during Harvester Battles to deal massive damage and/or destroy the harvester. You begin each game with **1 Lasgun** in your inventory (configurable 0–3). Lasgun shot difficulty is configurable: EASY (both right ramp and side ramp), HARD (only side ramp), or HYBRID (first two battles either ramp, then only side ramp).

## Thumper
Playfield multiplier. Press the **action button** to deploy all thumpers at once. Value increases the more Thumpers you have when you activate: 1 = 2x, 2 = 3x, 3 = 4x. Deploying thumpers also calls the worm, which approaches over ~20 seconds. As the worm approaches, a **worm jackpot** builds based on distance segments:

**Worm Distance Multiplier Table:**
| Segments | Multiplier |
|----------|-----------|
| 0–2 | 1x |
| 3–4 | 2x |
| 5–6 | 3x |
| 7–8 | 4x |
| 9–10 | 5x |
| 11–12 | 6x |
| 13–14 | 7x |
| 15–16 | 8x |
| 17–18 | 9x |
| 19–22 | 20x |
| 23 | 30x |

**Jackpot per segment:** 500,000 × distance multiplier × (active thumpers + 1)

You **cannot** use Thumpers during Prophecy modes.

**Immersive Thumper Pop:** By default (configurable), the pop bumper physically fires during thumper sequences to simulate the thumper's movement.

# Harvester Battle
There are 6 Harvester inserts on the playfield and you can battle one per day cycle. The **2nd Harvester** successfully destroyed awards an **extra ball** (configurable: 0–6).

Each battle adds more defenders and health to the Harvester. Soldier positions scale with battles completed:
- Battle 1: 1 soldier, 1 thopter
- Battle 2: 2 soldiers, 2 thopters
- Battle 3: 3 soldiers, 2 thopters
- Battle 4: 4 soldiers, 3 thopters
- Battle 5: 5 soldiers, 3 thopters
- Battle 6+: 6 soldiers, 4 thopters

Harvester health = 4 × (thopter count + soldier count).

Water is consumed during a battle. If water runs out before the Harvester is destroyed, the battle ends early and will need to be resumed the next day. A **bonus time** is given before water consumption begins, based on water level at battle start:
- Water > 50: 0 seconds bonus
- Water > 45: 5 seconds
- Water > 40: 10 seconds
- Water > 30: 20 seconds
- Otherwise: 30 seconds

## Battle Retry Mechanic
If a battle times out (Harvester not destroyed), the battle state is saved - soldier status, harvester health, thopter count, and battle phase. On the next day's battle, the game resumes from where you left off with the same bonus time calculation.

## Shot Types During Battle
- **Worm area:** Switches on the side and inside the Worm damage the Harvester (500,000 pts per hit).
- **Red shots:** Harkonnen soldiers defending the Harvester (750,000 pts per kill, 5 water per collect).
- **Yellow roving shots:** Ornithopters, also shown on the playfield screen (5,000,000 pts per kill).
- **Blue shots:** Use a Lasgun charge to deal massive damage.

The final yellow shot to destroy the Harvester requires the ball to make the jump into the area below the Harvester toy at the back. Alternatively, you can shoot a lit blue shot to use a Lasgun charge instead. Destroying the Harvester awards **7,500,000 pts**.

Once destroyed, the Worm will be called. During that time, you can collect any slain soldiers (blue shots) to collect water and feed them to the Worm. This increases the sacrifice jackpot reward (**1,000,000 per water**). If the Worm doesn't eat the ball before reaching the area, the sacrifice jackpot will be missed and the battle ends.

After completing a battle, the **Hunter-Seeker** becomes available.

# Prophecy Modes
Prophecy modes are only available during **night time** and are prevented during multiball.

The next mode will have its insert flashing yellow. You can change which mode is next by doing the **right orbit** to move forward, or **left orbit** to move backward through the mode list.

**Mode order:** Train → Survive → Ride → Rescue → Fight → Awaken

Each prophecy mode has a mid-point cash out option. At that point, you can choose to take the jackpot points offered and end the mode, or continue and risk it all for a **5x reward** as a hurry-up (minimum of half the cash out value) on the last shot. You also gain a **perk** for the rest of the game if you successfully shoot the hurry-up before it times out.

**Spice Jackpot:** When reaching the cash-out decision, all your spice is consumed. The jackpot value = max(spice consumed × 1,000,000, 5,000,000). The jackpot floor is half this value.

**Starting Points:** Each prophecy mode awards 1,000,000 + (200,000 × total modes started) at the beginning.

**Ball Save:** Configurable (default 10 seconds) at mode start, plus any earned ball save time.

**Scoop Start Difficulty** (configurable):
- **EASY:** Both front and rear scoop entry start modes
- **HARD:** Only front scoop entry starts modes
- **HYBRID:** First mode either entry, subsequent modes front only

Once completed, the prophecy mode insert will stay lit: **Green** if you cashed out at the mid-point, **Yellow** for full completion.

## Train
Spar with Gurney Halleck to hone your fighting skills.

Start by shooting a ball into the desert power field. Doing so will auto-plunge a second ball.

Bash the captive ball to spar until the meter is depleted. Base hit value: **12,990,000**. Each bash also scores **73,000** with a **1,260,000** boost.

Then shoot the center or right ramp to reach the mid-point cash out.

Continuing, bash the captive ball to spar until the meter is depleted. Once depleted, the captive ball will be released.

Shoot the lit shot to make a move or shoot the desert power field to move the lit shot.

Once made, send one ball back into the desert power field.

Complete this cycle of bashing the captive ball, making the lit shot and re-capturing a ball 3 times.

Then, left ramp will be lit. The hook will stop it and send it down the outlane.

Following this, the captive ball will release and a hurry-up jackpot will start at the center & right ramp to complete the mode.

**Perk:** Gurney will take out soldiers in Harvester Battle.

## Survive
Need to find the right pitch to compel the Harkonnen soldier to ungag Jessica.

A single shot is the correct one. All shots will be lit at the start. When a shot is made, if not the correct one, it will unlight all shots on the side of the playfield that doesn't contain the correct shot.

- Incorrect shot: **4,870,000 pts**
- Correct shot: **19,470,000 pts**
- Unlit shot: **250,000 pts**
- End Phase 1: **1,000,000 pts**
- Per altitude unit: **20,000 pts**

Once made, the right ramp will lower to trap the ball into the Sietch mountain for the mid-point cash out.

Continuing, Paul & Jessica need to reach 5000m of altitude to survive the storm. Ramp shots will be lit to gain altitude:
- Orange shot gains 1000m.
- Blue shot gains 500m.

**Perk:** One "The Voice" right outlane save per ball. These saves can stack - if you already have one and earn another, it's stored for future balls.

## Ride
Shoot lit shots to find the best Thumper location.

- Check sand: **3,750,000 pts**
- Set thumper: **18,740,000 pts**
- End Phase 1: **1,000,000 pts**
- Mount worm: **7,490,000 pts**
- Phase 4 shot: **2,500,000 pts** (max **12,500,000**)

Blue shot will represent the next Thumper location to try. Hitting the Right Ramp, Center spinner & Center ramp will find the right location. Once found, the Thumper bumper will be lit to place the Thumper. Next, Center ramp and Right ramp will be lit to reach the cash out.

If continuing, the Worm will be lit to start riding it. Worm will grab the ball and partially raise up, causing a second ball to be auto plunged. The Worm needs to be bashed 3–4 times, each hit will make the Worm raise up more. When enough shots are made, the right orbit will be lit to set the first hook. Second hook is set by making the left ramp. Ball will then be stopped by the hook and sent to drain.

Finally, Worm will complete its rise and release the previously captured ball onto the wireform. This activates a hurry-up at the center ramp & left ramp. Once made, the prophecy is completed with Paul riding Shai Hulud.

**Perk:** Thumper adds a ball once per Desert Power Multiball.

## Rescue
Rescue crew members by hitting lit targets then shoot the scoop to board the thopter!

All stand up targets are lit (7 total) representing crew members that need rescuing. Hit 2 targets to light a hurry-up at the scoop and to send the crew to safety on an ornithopter. Once a target is hit, it will not relight.

- Crew rescue: **980,000 pts**
- Board base: **2,940,000 pts**
- End Phase 1: **1,000,000 pts**

After the first set of 2 rescues & scoop shot, the Worm will light for the mid-point cash out option.

Continuing, the Worm will let go of the ball and you have to redo the 2 targets + scoop combo 3 more times.

**Perk:** Water consumption in Harvester Battle is slower.

## Fight
Complete the lit shots to help Paul face off against Jamis and join the Fremen.

- Shot base: **1,350,000 pts**
- Shot boost: **450,000 pts** per subsequent shot
- End Phase 1: **1,000,000 pts**

Flashing shot continues combo for an increased value. Note you do not need to shoot all shots as part of a combo.

Shoot lit shots to fight Jamis. Combo is Left ramp, right ramp, center ramp. Finishing the first part by shooting the center or right ramp to reach the mid-point cash out.

Continuing, new combo is Left ramp, Right ramp, 2x Center ramp, Middle spinner & desert power field. Finally, a hurry-up will start at the center & right ramp to complete the mode.

**Perk:** Double damage in Harvester Battles (damage multiplier = 2x).

## Awaken
Shoot any shot to attract the Worm, then strike again before it escapes.

**Phase 1 - Attract the Worm:** All 9 shots are available. Shoot any shot to lure the worm there (**370,000 pts** per lure). Then quickly shoot the same shot again before the worm flees. Successfully catching the worm scores **4,380,000 pts** (decreasing by **100,000** per catch). Repeat until enough catches are made.

**Phase 2 - Trap the Worm:** The side ramp is lit. Shoot the side ramp to trap the ball using the right post hold mechanic. The worm is now trapped.

**Phase 3 - Drown the Worm:** Shoot the pit/tunnel 5 times to drown the worm. Each hit scores **5,840,000 pts**. Then shoot the scoop to extract the water of life (**7,300,000 pts**). Finally, shoot the center or right ramp to receive the water of life. This is the mid-point cash out.

**Phase 4 - Fight Back:** If you continue, shoot all lit shots to fight back against the water of life (**1,780,000 pts** per shot). After all shots are made, center & right ramp will be lit for completing the mode.

**Perk:** Increased spice jackpot hurry-up time.

# Side Quests
## Feyd Rautha's Arena Battle
Qualified as the 4th Bene Gesserit award.

This is a major single-ball mode with a unique **defense system** and **3-shot combo** mechanic. The display switches to greyscale during the fight.

**Setup:** 5 shot arrows are used (left ramp, left orbit, center, side ramp, right orbit). Defense starts at **10**. The ramp is lowered and the diverter is set down.

**Phase 0 - Build Value:**
- 3 lit shots form a combo. Shoot any lit white arrow to start, then follow the combo path to the side ramp (via the right post trap mechanic), completing all 3 shots.
- Base shot value starts at **10,000,000** and increases by **10,000,000** per shot made.
- Defense acts as a scoring multiplier: points × (defense / 10). At full defense, you get 100% value.
- After completing the 3-shot combo, Phase 1 begins.

**Phase 1 - Final Round:**
- Same 3-shot combo system, but now shots score based on the total value accumulated in Phase 0 multiplied by combo position.
- Completing the combo wins the mode.

**Defense Mechanic:**
- Any **target hit** (standup targets, drop targets, pop bumper, sand trap target) reduces defense by **1** and scores **37,000 pts**.
- **Draining** reduces defense by **3**.
- At defense **0**, the mode ends in defeat.
- Ball save disappears when defense drops below **3** or after the first completed combo.
- Target reset time is configurable (default **6 seconds**).

**Flawless Victory:** If defense is still at **10** when you win, you receive a **100,000,000 pt** bonus.

**Completion:** Awards a **DUNE letter**.

**Pity Points:** Repeated/non-advancing shots score **75,000 pts** (÷10 if already made).

## Hunter-Seeker
Qualified by making the **jump ramp shot** (ball must hit the harvester switch after the jump ramp) when no Harvester Battle is ongoing and insert is flashing white (qualified after completing a battle).

The Hunter-Seeker appears as a single **roving lit shot** that moves from left to right across all 9 shot positions, spending **5 seconds** at each position. During the 1-second transition between positions, both the old and new positions are valid.

**Starting Value:** 20,000,000 + (10,000,000 × previous hunters caught)

**Value Decay:**
- Each **flipper button press** reduces value by **43,000** (with auto-repeat every 0.3 seconds while held)
- Each **target hit** reduces value by **32,000**

Shoot the lit shot to catch the Hunter-Seeker and collect the current value. If the Hunter-Seeker reaches the end (position 8) without being caught, it escapes and no points are awarded.

## Sandwalk Combo
The side ramp (both upper loop and lower exit) starts a sandwalk combo when lit gold. The left orbit also continues an active sandwalk. Starting value: **4,000,000 pts**, increasing by **250,000** each time.

After a sandwalk ends, there is a **1-second grace period** before it fully resets, and a **3-second timer** before the sandwalk relights. Incomplete sandwalks still add **half the base value** to the Run value.

### Run From Shai-Hulud
After completing a configurable number of sandwalks (default **3**, increases by 1 after each run up to a configurable maximum of **10**), the side ramp lights **solid gold** for a Run.

The Run has a configurable duration (default **30 seconds**). During the Run, make **5 side ramp shots** to complete it. The Run is a timed mode with its own overlay display; a warning video plays when 5 seconds remain. Double-flipping during the intro animation skips straight into the Run.

**Scoring:** Each of the 5 shots pays out the player's accumulated sandwalk value - the total of all sandwalk payouts banked since the last run. All 5 shots are worth the same amount, so the total payout is that value multiplied by 5. This bank grows throughout the game: the sandwalk base starts at 4,000,000 and increases by 250,000 each time a sandwalk payout is collected.

Completing the Run awards a **DUNE letter**. Failing to complete it within the time limit ends the mode with no letter awarded, though points for shots made are still kept.

The Run prevents Battles, Prophecy modes, Fedaykin Multiball, Desert Power, and DUNE wizard modes while active.

## Spice Dreams
While a ball is locked in the Sand Trap and Fedaykin Multiball is not running, hitting the **left inlane** qualifies a Spice Dream and the Sand Trap triangle insert flashes orange. You then have 5 seconds to shoot the ball into the Sand Trap to collect it. If the timer expires, the opportunity is lost.

**A collected Spice Dream awards:**
- Points scaled to your current spice amount and intensity level (minimum 1,000,000)
- A spotted shot in any active Battle or Prophecy mode
- A permanent increase to your maximum spice capacity (larger at higher intensity)
- A random video clip from a pool of 18 dream sequences, which cycle throughout the game

**Spice Intensity**

While a ball is locked, each hit on the Sand Trap drop target builds your Spice Intensity, up to a maximum of 6. Higher intensity means a bigger point award and a larger max spice bonus when the dream fires. Intensity resets to its base level after each dream collects.

**Spice Dreams are prevented during:** Battles, DUNE wizard modes, Voice From the Outer World, and Fedaykin Multiball.

# Bene Gesserit Awards
Mystery award collected at the left ramp each cycle between day/night (except first cycle). Left ramp will be lit purple when available.

Awards are drawn from 3 pools in a fixed rotation order: **Common, Common, Rare, Special** (then repeats). 

**Common Pool (Pool 0):**

7 awards, shuffled randomly each cycle (tournament mode skips the shuffle)

- Resources collection doubled during next day/night
- Spice filled to maximum capacity
- Water filled to maximum capacity
- Lowest resource filled to maximum
- Super spinners during next day/night
- Slower water consumption during next battle
- Desert Power jackpot values increased

**Rare Pool (Pool 1):**

3 awards, in fixed order (never shuffled)

- Extra Ball
- Light "The Voice" right outlane save
- Light the Pain Box test

**Special Pool (Pool 2):**

2 awards - both identical. The first time the cycle reaches Pool 2, instead of drawing from the pool, the player receives Feyd Rautha's Arena Fight. Once the arena mode completes, all future Pool 2 draws give the normal DUNE letter awards.

- Collect a DUNE letter
- Collect a DUNE letter


When a pool award would be useless (for example, spice filled to maximum capacity when spice is already maxed) it's swapped for points (drawn and consumed normally, pool still advances).

|Condition|Replaced with|
| --- | --- |
|DUNE Letter but already have 4 DUNE letters|5,000,000|
|Pain Box but already qualified|3,000,000|
|Max water but already at max water|1,000,000|
|Max spice but already at max spice|1,000,000|

# Multiballs
## Desert Power
Desert Power multiball locks can be activated by hitting the Thumper bumper. The number of thumper hits required increases with each multiball played: **(multiballs_played × 2) + 2** hits needed.

After the first Desert Power multiball, you will need to hit the Thumper item drop target (blue) to reactivate the Thumper bumper for lock activation. It will be flashing between blue and green to indicate this.

Once activated, the locks will be active at the left & right orbit shots. After 2 locks, the multiball will be qualified and can be started at the Worm. Lock value: **1,500,000 pts** each.

Note: By default (configurable), Desert Power multiball will be automatically qualified if not played before ball 3 is started.

### Build Phase
Before starting the multiball, you can increase the jackpot value by sending the ball into the desert power field (pit). The trapped ball becomes a bash toy.

- **Base jackpot:** 5,000,000
- **Pit wall switch hits** boost jackpot by **650,000** each
- **Spinner hits** during build boost by **100,000** each
- **Thumper bumper hits** during build score **250,000** each

The build phase lasts approximately **10 seconds**. After that, the worm path fills up and the message changes to "Shoot Worm To Start Multiball!" The jackpot value then begins to **decrease over time** (every 3 seconds) down to a floor equal to the starting jackpot value.

### Active Phase
Jackpot shots are lit orange. The "side" jackpot shots can be moved one shot over by using the switch in front of the Worm area. Hit the same switch repeatedly to send the jackpot all the way to the right or left for maximum multiplier. Each step moved from the starting location provides a multiplier (2x, 3x & 5x respectively). Shot will move back to the center after some time if the switch is not hit.

Once 3 jackpots are collected, the super jackpot phase will be qualified. To start it, you need to send a ball in the desert power field. You then need to use the other ball(s) to bash the captive ball to build the super jackpot. This will deplete a meter on screen. Once it is empty, the captive ball will be released. Collect the super jackpot at the spinner in the desert power field. Hitting it by entering the desert power field from the front will provide a 2x multiplier. The value of the super jackpot will be the value of the 3 jackpots collected combined. Super jackpot bash time is configurable (default **20 seconds**).

Once the super jackpot has been collected, the 3 jackpot shots will be relit to do the cycle again, as long as at least 2 balls remain in play.

## Fedaykin
2-ball Multiball at the middle-right kickback lane (Sand Trap).

The drop target must be hit first to allow access to the Sand Trap. For the first Fedaykin multiball, only one hit is required. Each subsequent time, one additional drop target hit is required. The number required is shown by the number of lit red bars behind the drop target. Lock difficulty is configurable (EASY: always 1 hit, MEDIUM: max 2, HARD: max 3).

The first ball into the Sand Trap will be locked and another ball will auto-plunge. Once locked, the kickback/ball must be hit twice more to qualify the FMB, as indicated by the light bars at the Sand Trap entrance. For each FMB after the first, the number of shots into the Sand Trap increases by 1. This is locked out during Prophecy modes, as indicated by all the Sand Trap light bars being lit.

Once qualified, the FMB light will flash. Hitting the Sand Trap once more will start FMB and release the locked ball.

**Ball Count:** Starts with 2 balls. The number of balls launched increases based on harvesters destroyed (up to 6 total).

**Ball Save:** Configurable (default 10 seconds).

In Fedaykin Multiball, the Sand Trap will be lit. Shooting the Sand Trap will do three things: lock the ball, light the Sand Trap shot green, and light the Fedaykin Jackpot at the upper right ramp. Shooting the Sand Trap while lit green will release the locked ball and autoplunge an **add-a-ball** (up to 3 added). The add-a-ball opportunity has a **12-second timeout** with a warning sound at 10.2 seconds.

**Fedaykin Jackpot:** Lit at the upper right ramp after shooting the Sand Trap. The first Fedaykin Jackpot is lit immediately on the first FMB and only requires one side ramp hit. **The first jackpot awards a DUNE letter**. Each subsequent Fedaykin Jackpot must be hit twice to score, and the score increases for each jackpot awarded.

- Ball caught: **1,500,000 pts**
- Ball added: **2,500,000 pts**
- Base jackpot: **5,000,000 pts**
- Jackpot per ball in play: **3,000,000 pts**

**Lock Stealing:** In multiplayer games (configurable, default on), a missing Sand Trap lock has to be re-locked.

# Pain Box
Left outlane ball save opportunity. Is automatically available at the start of ball 2 by default (configurable: 0–10, 0 to disable). Note: a loaded Sand Snork will take precedence over the Pain Box and immediately save your ball, leaving your Pain Box to be used on a future left outlane drain.

When active, visible with the purple insert above the left outlane, will trigger the ball to be captured into the apron. Player needs to hold the action button and keep it pressed until proven worthy.

**Cheat Detection:** Flipping both flippers within **120ms** of each other results in instant failure (detects rubber bands or clamps on the action button).

A new ball will be auto-plunged and the player needs to successfully make the left ramp shot. If successful, the new ball will be drained by the left wireform hook, and the previously trapped ball will be launched back onto the playfield. If unsuccessful, you have been proven unworthy and your ball ends.

Kickback ball save time is configurable (default **3 seconds**).

# DUNE Letters
You need to light all four DUNE letters to qualify each mini-wizard mode. Once a mini-wizard mode has been played, you need to relight the letters again. The mini-wizard modes (4 in total) are played in order (or selectable - see DUNE Mode Selection below). Each letter scores **750,000 pts**.

You gain a letter by:
- Destroying a Harvester
- Reaching the second phase of a Prophecy mode by choosing to "Continue"
- Collecting your first super jackpot during Desert Power Multiball
- Collecting your first jackpot during Fedaykin Multiball
- Bene Gesserit award
- Completing Pain Box
- Completing the Arena Fight
- Completing a Run from Shai-Hulud

# Mini-Wizard Modes
## DUNE Mode Selection
When enabled (configurable, default on), and fewer than 3 DUNE modes have been played, the player can **choose which DUNE mode to play** (Battle of Arrakeen, Fall of Sietch Tabr, or Liberate Arrakis) using flipper buttons and the action button. A **9-second timer** counts down (resets on input). In tournament mode or when only one option remains, selection is skipped and the next mode in order is played automatically.

The 4th DUNE mode is always Voice From the Outer World.

## Battle of Arrakeen
The Harkonnens are attacking Arrakeen to eliminate the Atreides.

- Single-ball mode
- All 6 shots are lit. Shoot each one twice in a row to collect them all (lock-in value: **730,000 pts** per shot). Note the ball will be held after each shot collected.
- Once all shots have been collected, a hurry-up starts at **43,960,000 pts** that decreases over time.
- Shoot the Harvester (jump shot at the back of the Worm) to complete the mode and collect the hurry-up value.
- Completing the mode awards a DUNE letter.

## Fall of Sietch Tabr
Hunt Harkonnen in the blowing sand. Save as many Fremen as you can.

**Phase 1 - Hunt Harkonnen:**
Making a spinner shot will clear the sand for some time, unveiling a lit shot to kill a Harkonnen soldier. Continue shooting spinners before making the lit shot to increase its value (**93,000 per spin** with **18,000 boost** per subsequent spin). The value is visible on the playfield screen. The lit shot will move from right to left for each ~3 spins. Each time sands cover back the playfield for the same Harkonnen soldier, the value per spin will decrease. Base points per spin: **3,700**.

You need to kill **6 soldiers** by making the lit shot before moving to the next phase.

**Phase 2 - Retreat:**
After these 6 kills, the Harkonnen are retreating. Head for the Sietch at the right orbit followed by the center or right ramp.

**Phase 3 - Save Fremen:**
Shoot any shot (Right ramp, desert power field front or back, Center ramp) that goes into the Sietch area to save as many Fremen as you can (**370,000 pts** per Fremen with **5,000 boost**). Each switch hit & spinner spin inside the desert power field will save 1 Fremen. Do this as many times as possible before time runs out (**30 seconds**) for the mode completion.

## Liberate Arrakis
Unleash desert power on the Harkonnens and Sardaukar. Take down the Baron. Jackpot values are based on your performance in Desert Power and Fedaykin Multiballs.

**Phase 1 - Call the Worms:**
Shoot the thumper to attract worms. Each thumper hit scores an escalating value - 3,000,000 for the first shot, increasing by 3,000,000 with each successive hit (6M, 9M, 12M...). Once the first thumper shot has been made, a 30-second timer starts. During that time, shoot the thumper as many times as possible. The first shot attracts 5 worms, with each additional thumper shot attracting another. A maximum of 9 worms can be attracted; hitting the maximum immediately ends Phase 1.

**Phase 2 - Ride Into Battle:**
Once worms are attracted, shoot the worm to ride into battle. The worm will grab the ball and rise up to deposit it onto the wireform. A 3-ball multiball will be started.

**Phase 3 - Collect All Jackpots:**
All shots are lit red for jackpots. The jackpot base value equals the highest super jackpot you achieved in Desert Power Multiball (minimum 20,000,000). Shot multipliers are based on position - outer shots pay the most:

- **Left Spinner / Right Orbit: 5× jackpot**
- **Left Ramp / Side Ramp: 3× jackpot**
- **Scoop / Tunnel: 2× jackpot**
- **Left Orbit / Worm / Center: 1× jackpot**

The active jackpot window shifts across the playfield over time and can be steered left or right using the standup targets. Collect all jackpots to progress.

**Phase 4 - Start the Fedaykin Attack:**
Shoot the sand trap to launch the Fedaykin. A bonus equal to half the accumulated thumper value from Phase 1 is awarded when the sand trap is made.

**Phase 5 - Fedaykin Attack:**
Defeat 10 Harkonnens to move on. Shoot lit red Harkonnen shots to score. Each kill is worth half the jackpot base value multiplied by the number of balls currently in play - the jackpot base equals the highest jackpot you achieved in Fedaykin Multiball (minimum 10,000,000). The sand trap adds a ball and relights all Harkonnen shots (up to 5 additional balls); after the maximum balls are added, the sand trap instead relights shots without adding balls. Sand trap value: **112,500 pts**.

**Phase 6 - Break Into the Throne Room:**
After 10 Harkonnens have been defeated, shoot the worm 3 times to break down the door. Each shot scores an escalating value: **50,000,000** for the first, **75,000,000** for the second, and **100,000,000** for the third. After the third shot, shoot the Harvester to break through, which scores **200,000,000 pts**. Once the 4th shot is locked underneath the Harvester, the flippers go dead to drain down to one ball.

**Phase 7 - Defeat the Baron:**
Once the remaining ball is released, a hurry-up starts at the left orbit. The hurry-up begins at half the total jackpots collected throughout the mode and counts down over 20 seconds to a floor of 10% of its starting value. Shoot the left orbit to collect and defeat the Baron.

## Voice From the Outer World
The final wizard mode. Paul must defeat Feyd-Rautha in single combat.

**Setup:** 2-ball minimum is maintained throughout. All other game systems are prevented (resources, time, items, battles, prophecies, etc.).

**Health System:** Both Paul and Feyd start with **15 health points**.

**Phase 1 - Trap the Ball:**
7 shots are available (right ramp, left ramp, right orbit, side ramp, center shot, upper loop, tunnel). Shoot the pit to trap the ball and begin the fight sequence.

**Phase 2 - Fight:**
While the ball is trapped in the pit, switches inside the pit add time to the release timer. Targets on the playfield damage Feyd. The "double shot" mechanic rewards consecutive hits to the same shot type with bonus damage. Feyd damage value: **50,000,000 pts** per instance.

When the ball is released from the pit, you must trap it again to continue fighting. The ball can go back and forth between Phase 1 and Phase 2.

**Damage to Paul:**
- Each **drain** costs Paul **1 health point**
- **Single ball play** (both balls drain) costs Paul **3 health points**

**Targets:** Standup targets pulse orange and damage Feyd. Target reset time is configurable (default 6 seconds).

**Phase 3 - Hurry-Up:**
When either fighter reaches low health, the fight intensifies. A hurry-up activates with a starting value of **1,000,000,000 pts** and a floor of **500,000,000 pts** over **30 seconds**.

**Game End Condition:** Completing Voice mode (defeating Feyd) clears any extra balls the winning player had earned. If every player in the game has now played Voice mode (whether they won or lost) the game advances to its final state: the current ball becomes the last ball for all players, and the game ends normally after it drains.

# Bonus
End of ball bonus is calculated as:

**(Total Water × 13,000 + Total Spice × 22,000) × Multiplier**

**Multiplier:** Starts at 100%. Each day/night transition during the ball adds **+50%** to the multiplier, capped at **8 transitions = 500%** (effectively 5x bonus).

**Double-flip** (both flippers simultaneously) skips the bonus display.

# Extra Balls
- 2nd Harvester Battle won (configurable: 0–6, default 2)
- Bene Gesserit award (from the Rare pool)
- Replay score (configurable - not by default, need to change in settings)
- Extra ball limit applies - once reached, extra balls award **25,000,000 pts** instead

Note if this causes the player to pass the scoring threshold for an extra ball, the poison tooth is available on that ball as well. This is disabled in tournament mode.

# Alternate Game Modes
## Barrel Ball
Barrels of Fun's implementation of "stall ball" - A party/competitive elimination game mode, accessible from the gameplay modes menu (hold right flipper during attract mode). Must be enabled in settings (default: on).

**Setup:** Players set a player count using the flipper buttons. The game uses a single ball with special drain handling - draining counts as an elimination but launches a new ball.

**10 Shot Locations:** left spinner, left ramp, scoop, left orbit, worm, tunnel, side ramp, pit, right orbit, sand trap.

**Starting Layout:**
- **Red shots** (dangerous): tunnel
- **White shots** (safe/stall): left ramp, left orbit, worm, right orbit, pit, sand trap
- **Dead shots** (unlit): left spinner, scoop, side ramp

**Mechanics:**
- **White shots** "stall" the ball - the ball is caught at the location (via magnets, posts, or scoop) and held briefly before release.
- **Red shots** eliminate the active player (configurable: can be set to just stall instead).
- **Draining** also eliminates the active player.
- The game continues until only one player remains.

**Tim Kitzrow Commentary:** Configurable voice callouts provide color commentary on the game (default: on).

No standard gameplay systems are active during Barrel Ball - no resources, no time system, no modes.
